Chicken Curry

Description

When I think of comfort food, this chicken curry comes to mind. I remember eating this on cold winter days atop of a nice warm bowl of freshly made rice. It's hearty with potatoes and carrots to fill you up, with just enough red curry spice to feel a kick. A citrusy blend of lemongrass and lemon leaves, and our star player, galangal, will fill your home with a wonderful delicious aroma.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 medium/large potatoes
  • 2 large carrots
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 pack of chicken thighs bone-in (4-5 pieces)
  • 1 can of red spicy curry paste
  • 1-2 3-inch stalks of lemongrass
  • 2 lemon leaves
  • Galangal cut into a 2-inch knob
  • 1 can of coconut milk or cream
  • Water to submerge chickens in completely
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped cilantro and green onions, to serve

Directions

  1. Cut potatoes to 1 inch pieces. Slice carrots to edible size
  2. Heat pot over med-high heat, add oil to cover bottom. Once hot, add in garlic, curry paste, lemongrass, lemon leaves, and galangal. Stir well until fragant ~1-2 minutes
  3. Once fragrant, add chicken and mix to cover in paste
  4. Add in coconut milk/cream. Add enough water to completely submerge chicken
  5. Once boiling, add potatoes and carrots. Cover and let sit until chicken is cooked ~30-40 minutes. Be careful not to overcook the potatoes
  6. Season to taste
  7. When chicken is cooked, optional to take out and shred, otherwise serve whole
  8. Serve atop rice and garnish with cilantro and green onions
